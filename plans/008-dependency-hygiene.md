## Why this matters

Two libraries are imported directly by the engine but are not declared in
`pyproject.toml` — they only resolve today because a transitive dependency
happens to pull them in (`python-dotenv` via `pydantic-settings`,
`pandas` via `python-jobspy`). The day either transitive chain drops or
re-pins its sub-deps, `engine/cli.py` and `engine/discovery/jobspy_source.py`
break at import time with no warning. Declaring them as direct deps makes the
dependency contract honest and import-stable. In the other direction, six
declared dependencies (three Python, three frontend) are dead weight: a
repo-wide grep finds zero usages. They are vestigial from a pre-reportlab
document-generation approach and an abandoned drag-reorder UI. Removing them
shrinks the lockfiles, the install surface, and the audit/CVE blast radius.

### Conventions that apply here

- Python deps are pinned with `>=` floors in `pyproject.toml` (see every line
  in the excerpt above). Match that style for the two additions.
- The repo uses **uv** for Python (`uv.lock` present) and **npm** for the
  frontend (`package-lock.json` present). Do not introduce pip, poetry, pnpm,
  or yarn.
- Floors for the two new deps must match what uv already resolved in `uv.lock`
  (see Step 2). Do not invent a floor higher than the installed version.
